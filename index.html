Same card. Same answer. Three different lenses.

The fraction slice is deliberately small: one card, one wrong answer, one misconception, and one next step. The point is that every layer can be inspected.

For the learner, the system should feel calm and doable. There is no graph jargon here: just a precise card, tactile fraction bars, a hint when needed, and feedback that stays focused on the maths.

pondera.principles

Why structured checking matters

Many systems can respond conversationally. Pondera is designed around checked learning events: visible cards, structured answers, deterministic checkers, explicit evidence, and inspectable next-step reasoning.

Every card knows what it teaches

Practice is tied to explicit knowledge points, misconceptions, representations, and prerequisites in the graph.

Every answer is checked by rules

Versioned deterministic checkers decide correctness. The same answer gets the same verdict every time.

Every misconception is traceable

A wrong answer can activate a named misconception with a confidence score and a concrete failure signal.

Every next step has a reason

The sequencer can explain why it chose review, remediation, or new material from the learner model.

pondera.misconception.fractions

Misconception-specific remediation

Many systems can track whether an answer was right or wrong. Pondera is designed to notice what kind of wrong answer it was.

  1. 01Learner is asked 1/4 + 2/4 as a diagnostic task.
  2. 02They answer 3/8 - a known misconception pattern.
  3. 03The checker flags the misconception and logs evidence.
  4. 04The sequencer selects a visual remediation, not another guess.
  5. 05Learner writes 3/4, and the misconception confidence drops.

pondera.sequencer.placement

Always working at the edge of what you know

Pondera places the learner where the evidence says they are, not simply where their age or grade says they should be.

placement

Placed by evidence, not age

A learner can be strong in one strand and shaky in another. The graph lets those states differ without forcing a single grade label.

inference

Prerequisites inferred, not just tested

Strong downstream performance can provide evidence that prerequisites are probably solid, without testing every prerequisite directly.

frontier

Review and new ground, balanced

The sequencer should balance revision with pushing the boundary forward, keeping practice near the frontier of readiness.

Like spaced repetition, but over a curriculum graph. Instead of scheduling isolated flashcards, Pondera uses evidence from connected skills to decide what to review, repair, or introduce next.
pondera.notes

What makes the system inspectable

The alpha slice is small, but the architectural promise is concrete: each visible learning moment has a structured trail behind it.

LayerWhat is visibleWhy it matters
Evidence trail

The card, answer, checker rule, diagnostic signal, and next step can be inspected together.

A parent, tutor, or technical reviewer can ask why something happened and get a structured answer.

AI boundary

Hints and explanations can be generated around checked events and graph facts.

The goal is not a black-box tutor, but an assistant wrapped around structured learning state.

This is an early implementation direction, not a finished-product claim.
