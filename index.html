Hint voice

Line up the pieces. You are adding fourths to fourths. Count how many fourths are shaded altogether.

No score shame. No random chat. Just the next useful nudge.

Plain-language interpretation

  • Charlie answered: 3/8
  • Likely misconception: added denominators
  • Understands: the numerator counts shaded pieces
  • Needs work: keeping the unit size when adding same units

Recommended next step

Return to a visual card where all pieces are the same size. Ask the learner to count fourths before writing the symbolic answer.

The map underneath

Every card sits somewhere in a map of maths knowledge.

This fraction example is small on purpose. The broader idea is a connected curriculum map: skills, prerequisites, misconceptions, cards, checkers, and evidence.

Cards map to skills

A task is not just a question. It is tied to the knowledge it is meant to practise or diagnose.

Mistakes can be specific

3/8 is not merely wrong. It points to a likely denominator-addition misconception.

Progress is evidence-based

The learner model changes because checked answers produced evidence, not because a chat model guessed.

Placement by evidence

Not just age. Not just grade. The edge of readiness.

Pondera should place the learner where the evidence says they are: move ahead when connected skills look strong, review when knowledge is fading, and rebuild ideas when a misconception appears.

Different strengths can coexist

A learner might be ready to move forward in one area while needing repair in another.

Prerequisites can be inferred

Strong downstream performance can count as evidence that earlier skills are probably solid.

Review and frontier are balanced

Like spaced repetition, but over a curriculum graph rather than isolated flashcards.
