Three faces of the same system

The student sees a focused learning card. Families and tutors see progress and next actions. Builders see the graph, cards, checkers, and evidence that make it trustworthy.

pondera.principles

Why deterministic

Adaptive tutoring usually means a model guessing at correctness and progress. Pondera separates the two questions: what's true, and how it's said.

Every card knows what it teaches

Practice is tied to explicit knowledge points, misconceptions, representations, and prerequisites in the graph.

Every answer is checked by rules

Versioned deterministic checkers decide correctness. The same answer gets the same verdict every time.

Every misconception is traceable

A wrong answer can activate a named misconception with a confidence score and a concrete failure signal.

Every next step has a reason

The sequencer can explain why it chose review, remediation, or new material from the learner model.

pondera.misconception.fractions

A misconception, caught and corrected

A learner adds denominators as well as numerators. Pondera catches the pattern and routes to visual remediation.

  1. 01Learner is asked 1/4 + 2/4 as a diagnostic task.
  2. 02They answer 3/8 - a known misconception pattern.
  3. 03The checker flags the misconception and logs evidence.
  4. 04The sequencer selects a visual remediation, not another guess.
  5. 05Learner writes 3/4, and the misconception confidence drops.

pondera.sequencer.placement

Always working at the edge of what you know

There's no grade level to place a learner at. There's only a graph, and evidence about where on it they currently stand.

placement

Placed by evidence, not age

A learner starts wherever their attempts say they are, and that can differ across strands.

inference

Prerequisites inferred, not just tested

Performing well on a knowledge point counts as evidence that its prerequisites are solid too.

frontier

Review and new ground, balanced

The sequencer mixes spaced review with new material at the frontier the learner has earned.

Like spaced repetition, but over a curriculum. Anki decides when you will forget a single fact. Pondera applies that idea across an entire graph of math knowledge.
pondera.notes

Where this differs from other adaptive math tools

Knowledge-graph-driven adaptive math platforms are not new. Two things we lean on harder than most:

IdeaWhat's commonWhat we emphasize
Misconception-level diagnosis

Most adaptive systems track mastery as a level or confidence score per topic.

Pondera diagnoses the specific error pattern and routes to remediation built for that misconception.

Where AI is allowed to act

Many systems use model-driven decision-making for what comes next.

Pondera keeps correctness, mastery, and sequencing inside deterministic, versioned, testable logic.

This is an architectural stance, not a finished claim of superiority. The system is still early.
